Deliverable 1: Creative Strategy Memo

Audience

UOB GWB leadership, transformation leads, operations heads, technology directors, risk and compliance leaders, servicing managers, collections/receivables team leads, and relationship management leadership across ASEAN markets.

Objective

Demonstrate that VocalIQ is a credible, governed, bank-grade AI co-worker for Global Wholesale Banking. The teaser must make the product immediately understandable to wholesale banking professionals and position it as an orchestration layer, not a chatbot.

Main Message

Every client conversation becomes structured, prioritized, traceable action.

Why This Teaser is Relevant to UOB GWB

UOB's GWB division serves corporations and institutions across ASEAN, Greater China, and beyond. The operating model involves Relationship Managers coordinating across servicing, trade, cash management, financial supply chain, and collections teams. Today, client requests arrive across calls, emails, meetings, and portals with no unified capture or routing layer. VocalIQ addresses this exact gap by connecting voice, email, and meeting intelligence into structured, controlled workflows that respect bank policy.

What Problem It Solves

For RMs: Fragmented priorities, manual meeting prep, incomplete client context, chasing servicing status.

For Servicing: Partial handoffs, missing context, manual classification, unclear SLA ownership.

For Collections/Receivables: Reactive exception management, delayed follow-ups, unclear prioritization, manual reconciliation tracking.

What Must Not Be Claimed

This is not an official UOB product. The teaser must not imply live deployment, approved integration, autonomous payment execution, AI-driven credit approval, or replacement of human bankers. No UOB logos, proprietary UI elements, or brand marks should be used without explicit authorization.

Risks and Mitigations

Risk: Perceived as consumer AI chatbot. Mitigation: Ground every scene in wholesale banking operations, use specific trade/cash/collections terminology.

Risk: Overpromising autonomous execution. Mitigation: Show human approval at every sensitive decision point. Scenes 12 and 13 are dedicated to controls and audit.

Risk: Brand confusion with UOB. Mitigation: Use "UOB-style" context without logos, slogans, or exact UOB interface elements. Label as concept/pilot teaser.

Motion: Subtle shimmer effects on panels, gentle pulsing on active indicators, smooth fade transitions between states.

Voiceover Generation Guidance

Voice character: Calm, senior narrator with executive authority. International business English with slight warmth. Not robotic. Not hype-driven.

Recommended tools: ElevenLabs (Adam or Daniel voice) or similar professional TTS with natural prosody. Licensed synthetic voice only. Do not clone any real person.

Pacing: Measured in scenes 1-2, confident and flowing in scenes 3-8, grounded and authoritative in scenes 9-13, expansive and resolved in scene 14.

Music: Premium corporate innovation score. Start subtle and tense, build to confident momentum. Recommend Artlist or Epidemic Sound corporate/innovation category. No aggressive tech beats.

Editing Guidance

Scenes 1-2: Faster cuts, slight visual tension. Cross-dissolves between fragmented tools.

Scene 3: Visual calm. Slow dissolve from chaos to organized workbench.

Scenes 4-8: Productive momentum. Clean cuts, steady pacing.

Scenes 9-11: Operational depth. Medium-paced, detail-oriented.

Scenes 12-13: Trust and control. Deliberate pacing, dramatic but restrained.

Scene 14: Confident executive close. Wide shots, slow camera movement, text overlays appear with gentle animation.

Transitions: Smooth dissolves between scenes. No hard cuts except in Scene 2 montage. UI elements can use slide-in or fade-in with slight easing.

Deliverable 6: Compliance and Brand Safety Checklist

  • UOB name/logo: NOT used unless client provides explicit brand approval
  • Product positioned as concept/pilot/internal teaser unless authorized otherwise
  • No false claims about live UOB integration or deployment
  • No autonomous payment execution shown
  • No AI approving credit, changing beneficiary details, or moving funds
  • No real customer data displayed (all sample data is fictional)
  • No real client names (Orion Manufacturing, Pacific Components, etc. are fictional)
  • No copied UOB Infinity or internal UOB interface elements
  • No cloned employee or executive voices
  • No AI impersonating a named UOB employee
  • No investment, treasury, or hedging advice shown as final recommendation
  • No AI negotiating debt or collections commitments without human supervision
  • Human approval shown at every sensitive decision point
  • Authentication requirements clearly visible before sensitive disclosure
  • Supervisor takeover capability demonstrated
  • Audit trail shown as complete and traceable
  • No consumer branch or retail app visuals
  • No quantified performance claims unless validated by pilot data
